Based on the design mockups provided, I have analyzed the underlying structure of the website for Sonia Rose. The site is a cohesive, multi-page real estate portfolio and service website.

The design relies heavily on a Component-Based Architecture, meaning many sections (headers, footers, lead magnet forms) are reused across different pages. The aesthetic is minimalist, using earth tones (beiges, browns) and serif typography to convey elegance and a human-centric approach.

Here is the global high-level overview of the pages and their structural breakdown, mapped against your uploaded files.

1. Global Design System (Shared Assets)
Before diving into specific pages, these elements exist globally across the site:

Typography:

Headings: Elegant Serif font (e.g., "TRAVAILLER AVEC MOI").

Body: Clean Sans-Serif font (e.g., "Agence immobilière...").

Color Palette: Beige backgrounds (#f5f5f0 approx), Dark Brown buttons/text (#5a4a42 approx), White cards, and standard Re/Max branding colors (Blue/Red) restricted to logos.

Footer (Found in all files):

Left: Logo (Sonia Rose) + Phone/Email.

Center: Re/Max Logo.

Right: Social Media Icons (Insta, FB, TikTok, LinkedIn).

French Text to keep: "Agence immobilière - Franchisé indépendant et autonome de Re/max Québec", "Tous droits réservés."
